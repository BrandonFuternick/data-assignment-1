SAVE OUR STAGES:
Keeping Entertainment Venues Alive in a Pandemic

By Brandon Futernick

When COVID-19 hit the U.S. in March of this year, live entertainment venues were the first businesses forced to shut down completely. With no revenue in over half a year and no clear end in sight, these venues their employees are struggling to make ends meet until they can reopen.


According to the National Independent Venue Association (NIVA), the live music industry produces upwards of $20 billion in annual revenue a year. On top of their own revenue, these venues attract business to bars, hotels, restaurants, and shops in their local areas.


The 'Save our Stages Act' was introduced in the Senate in July by Senators John Cornyn (TX) and Amy Klobuchar (MN). If enacted, the bill would provide up to $12 million in grants to any individual "eligible live venue operator, promoter, producer, or talent representative" that is affected by the shutdown. The Senate has been on several recesses since the bill was introduced and failed to even hold a vote on it.


Senator Chuck Schumer (NY) has been an outspoken proponent of the 'Save Our Stages Act', holding press conferences with prominent performers such as comedian Jerry Seinfeld and musician James Murphy. A country-wide stunt called #RedAlert was held last month, which featured hundreds of impacted venues turning their marquees red to create a real-life map showing the widespread effect of the pandemic closures.


To help keep venues alive, visit SaveOurStages.com and sign up to send an email to your Senators urging them to support the 'Save Our Stages Act' and give live venues a desperately needed lifeline in these uncertain times.
